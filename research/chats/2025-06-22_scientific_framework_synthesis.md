An analogous scheme uses colors that are adjacent on the color wheel, such as blue, green, and yellow, resulting in a harmonious and pleasing look. A complementary scheme uses colors opposite on the color wheel, like red and green or purple and yellow, creating a strong and dynamic look. A triadic scheme uses colors evenly spaced on the color wheel, such as red, yellow, and blue, for a balanced and vibrant look. A sequential scheme uses colors that vary in lightness or saturation along a single hue, such as light to dark blue. This is suitable for ordinal or quantitative data like rankings or percentages.

A diverging scheme uses two contrasting colors that diverge from a neutral midpoint, such as red and blue from white. This works best with bipolar or relative data like positive and negative values or deviations from a baseline. Finally, a qualitative scheme uses distinct and unrelated colors like red, green, blue, and yellow for categorical or nominal data like groups or labels.
