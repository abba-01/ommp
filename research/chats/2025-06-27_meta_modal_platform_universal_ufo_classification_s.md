## A Universal Observational Taxonomy for Anomalous Aerial, Aquatic, and Terrestrial Phenomena

### Abstract

The Meta Modal Platform (MMP) Framework presents a paradigmatic shift from ambiguous, culturally-loaded terminology (UFO, UAP, USO) to a scientifically rigorous, observationally-based classification system. This three-tiered taxonomy addresses the fundamental challenge of linguistic bias in anomalous phenomena reporting while maintaining accessibility across diverse observer populations. The framework employs a structured notation system: [(body)]M(context)KS, where Meta represents manifestation domain, Modal indicates kinematic behavior, and Platform describes morphological characteristics. This approach eliminates implicit cultural assumptions while preserving observational fidelity across terrestrial and extraterrestrial environments.

### 1. Introduction and Problem Statement

#### 1.1 The Linguistic Burden of Current Terminology
Current phenomenological classification relies heavily on culturally-embedded terms that carry implicit assumptions about origin, intent, and technology. The term "Unidentified Flying Object" presupposes aerial locomotion and artificial construction, while "Unidentified Aerial Phenomena" maintains atmospheric bias. These linguistic constraints create systematic observation errors and database incompatibilities.

#### 1.2 The Observer Paradox in Anomalous Phenomena
Multiple observers of identical phenomena frequently report contradictory characteristics, creating irreconcilable database entries. Traditional systems force false consensus, losing critical observational data. The MMP Framework preserves observational plurality while enabling pattern recognition across disparate reports.

#### 1.3 Scientific Objectives
- Develop culturally-neutral classification methodology
- Preserve observational fidelity across diverse observer populations
- Enable computational analysis of historical and contemporary reports
- Establish framework scalability for extraterrestrial observations

### 3. Methodology: The Meta Modal Platform Framework

#### 3.1 Core Architecture

**3.1.1 Meta Domain Classification (M)**
Eight primary manifestation environments:
- T (Terrestrial): Ground-based phenomena
- A (Atmospheric): Aerial manifestations
- L (Liquid): Aquatic/subaquatic phenomena
- O (Orbital): Space-based observations
- I (Interplanetary): Solar system phenomena
- S (Stellar): Stellar-distance observations
- G (Galactic): Galactic-scale phenomena
- U (Undetermined): Insufficient localization data

**3.1.2 Modal Kinematic States (K)**
Eight motion classifications based on measurable parameters:
- Z (Zero): Stationary (|v| < 0.1 m/s)
- L (Linear): Constant velocity trajectory
- C (Curved): Predictable arc motion (0.1 < |a| < 10 m/s²)
- E (Erratic): Unpredictable acceleration (|a| > 10 m/s²)
- R (Reactive): Observer-responsive behavior
- P (Periodic): Temporal pattern repetition
- O (Oscillating): Pendulum/wave motion
- U (Undetermined): Insufficient kinematic data

Fourteen geometric classifications:
- T (Triangle), V (Chevron), R (Rectangle), C (Cube)

- (f) Flight-level observation with altitude
- (s) Surface-level observation

- Body prefixes for extraterrestrial observations
